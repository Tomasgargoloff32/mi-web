Number and language

Core Extended + Chapter 1 Page 4 Pimentel & Wall, 4th ed.
Core & Extended — E1.1
E1.1 Identify and use natural numbers, integers (positive, negative and zero), prime numbers, square and cube numbers, common factors and common multiples, rational and irrational numbers (e.g. π, √2), real numbers, and reciprocals.
C
E1.3 Calculate with squares, square roots, cubes and cube roots and other powers and roots of numbers.
C
E1.4 Use directed numbers in practical situations.
C
Key vocabulary and number types
Natural numbers ℕ
Integers ℤ
Rational numbers ℚ
Irrational numbers
Real numbers ℝ
Prime numbers
Square numbers
Cube numbers
HCF / LCM
Reciprocal
Directed numbers
Key definitions
ℕ = {0, 1, 2, 3, 4, …}
ℤ = {…, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, …}
ℚ = all numbers of the form p/q (q ≠ 0)
Irrational: cannot be written as p/q (e.g. π, √2, √3)
Reciprocal of x = 1/x (e.g. reciprocal of 4 = 0.25)
★
All integers are rational (e.g. 5 = 5/1), but not all rationals are integers. Irrational numbers have decimal expansions that never terminate and never repeat.

Accuracy

Core Extended + Chapter 2Page 13
Core — E1.9
E1.9 Make estimates of numbers, quantities and lengths. Give approximations to specified numbers of significant figures and decimal places and round off answers to reasonable accuracy in the context of a given problem.
C
Core & Extended — E1.10
E1.10 Give appropriate upper and lower bounds for data given to a specified accuracy.
C
Ext only Obtain appropriate upper and lower bounds to solutions of simple problems given data to a specified accuracy (error intervals in calculations).
Key concepts
Rounding rules
3.456 to 2 d.p. → 3.46
3.456 to 2 s.f. → 3.5
0.007821 to 2 s.f. → 0.0078

Bounds: x = 4.3 (1 d.p.)
Lower bound = 4.25, Upper bound = 4.35
★
For Extended: when adding two measured values, add their upper bounds for the maximum result, and add their lower bounds for the minimum result. For subtraction, use upper − lower for maximum and lower − upper for minimum.

Indices and standard form

Core Extended + Chapter 7Page 62
Core & Extended — E1.7
E1.7 Understand the meaning of indices (fractional, negative and zero) and use the rules of indices. Use the standard form A × 10ⁿ where n is a positive or negative integer, and 1 ≤ A < 10.
C
Laws of indices
Core rules (all candidates)
aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ
aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ
(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
a⁰ = 1
a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ
Extended — fractional indices
a^(1/n) = ⁿ√a
a^(m/n) = (ⁿ√a)ᵐ = ⁿ√(aᵐ)

e.g. 27^(2/3) = (³√27)² = 3² = 9
Standard form
3 500 000 = 3.5 × 10⁶
0.00042 = 4.2 × 10⁻⁴

Condition: 1 ≤ A < 10

Algebraic representation and manipulation

Core Extended + Chapter 11Page 102
Core — E2.1 / E2.2
E2.1 Use letters to express generalised numbers and express basic arithmetic processes algebraically. Substitute numbers for words and letters in formulae. Rearrange simple formulae. Construct simple expressions and set up simple equations.
C
E2.2 Manipulate directed numbers. Use brackets and extract common factors. Expand products of algebraic expressions.
C
Ext only Factorise where possible expressions of the form: ax + bx + kay + kby; a²x² − b²y²; a² + 2ab + b²; ax² + bx + c. Manipulate algebraic fractions.
Factorising — Extended
Key factorisation types
Common factor: 6x² + 9x = 3x(2x + 3)
Difference of squares: a² − b² = (a+b)(a−b)
Perfect square: x² + 6x + 9 = (x+3)²
Trinomial: x² + 7x + 12 = (x+3)(x+4)
Grouping: ax + ay + bx + by = (a+b)(x+y)

Straight line graphs

Core Extended + Chapter 21Page 250
Core & Extended — E3.1–E3.4
E3.1 Demonstrate familiarity with Cartesian coordinates in two dimensions. Interpret and obtain the equation of a straight-line graph in the form y = mx + c.
C
E3.2 Calculate the gradient of a straight line from the coordinates of two points on it.
C
E3.3–E3.4 Calculate the length and the coordinates of the midpoint of a line segment from the coordinates of its endpoints.
C
Ext only Know that the gradient of parallel lines is equal. Know that the gradients of perpendicular lines multiply to give −1. Find the equation of a line parallel or perpendicular to a given line.
Key formulas
All candidates
Gradient: m = (y₂−y₁) / (x₂−x₁)
Equation: y = mx + c
Midpoint: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2 , (y₁+y₂)/2)
Length: d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²)
Extended only
Parallel lines: m₁ = m₂
Perpendicular lines: m₁ × m₂ = −1

Perimeter, area and volume

Core Extended + Chapter 27Page 351
Core — E5.2 Formulae
E5.2 Carry out calculations involving the perimeter and area of a rectangle, triangle, parallelogram and trapezium, and compound shapes derived from these.
C
E5.3 Carry out calculations involving the circumference and area of a circle, arc length and area of sector.
C
E5.4–E5.5 Surface area and volume of cuboid, prism, cylinder. Surface area and volume of sphere, pyramid and cone.
C
Ext only Solve problems involving the surface area and volume of a sphere, pyramid and cone. Apply to compound 3D shapes.
Formula reference
2D shapes
Rectangle: A = lw
Triangle: A = ½bh
Parallelogram:A = bh
Trapezium: A = ½(a+b)h
Circle: A = πr², C = 2πr
Sector: A = (θ/360)πr², arc = (θ/360)2πr
3D shapes
Cuboid: V = lwh
Cylinder: V = πr²h, SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
Sphere: V = (4/3)πr³, SA = 4πr²
Cone: V = (1/3)πr²h, SA = πrl + πr²
Pyramid: V = (1/3) × base area × h

Trigonometry

Core Extended + Chapter 29Page 394
Core — E6.2 Right-angled triangles
E6.2 Interpret and use three-figure bearings. Apply Pythagoras' theorem and the trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, tan) to the calculation of a side or of an angle of a right-angled triangle.
C
Ext only Know and use sine rule and cosine rule for any triangle. Find the area of a triangle using: A = ½ab sin C.
E6.3 Solve trigonometric problems in 2D involving angles of elevation and depression.
C
Formulas
Right-angled triangles (all candidates)
sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
tan θ = opposite / adjacent

Pythagoras: a² + b² = c²
Sine & cosine rules — Extended
Sine rule: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C
Cosine rule: a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A
Area: A = ½ ab sin C

Ch 33 · Probability
Topic 8 · Probability  ·  E8.1–E8.3

Probability

Core Extended + Chapter 33Page 476
Core — E8.1–E8.3
E8.1 Calculate the probability of a single event as either a fraction, decimal or percentage. Understand and use the probability scale 0 to 1.
C
E8.2 Understand that the probability of an event not occurring = 1 − P(event occurring). Use possibility diagrams and tree diagrams.
C
Ext only Calculate conditional probability using Venn diagrams, tree diagrams and tables. Understand and apply P(A|B).
Key rules
Core rules
0 ≤ P(A) ≤ 1
P(not A) = 1 − P(A)
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) [mutually exclusive]
P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) [independent]
Conditional probability — Extended
P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)

Without replacement: probabilities change
on second draw — use tree diagrams.

Statistics

Core Extended + Chapters 35–37Page 504
Core — E9.3 Averages
E9.3 Calculate the mean, median, mode and range for individual and discrete data, and distinguish between the purposes for which they are used.
C
Ext only Calculate an estimate of the mean for grouped and continuous data. Identify the modal class from grouped continuous data.
Core — E9.1, E9.2 Collecting and displaying
E9.1 Collect, classify and tabulate statistical data. Read, interpret and draw inferences from tables and statistical diagrams. Construct and use bar charts, pie charts, pictograms, stem-and-leaf diagrams, simple frequency distributions.
C
Ext only Construct and interpret histograms with equal and unequal intervals (frequency density). Construct scatter diagrams, draw lines of best fit, understand correlation types, use line of best fit to predict.
Extended — E9.4 Cumulative frequency
E9.4 Construct and interpret cumulative frequency tables and diagrams. Estimate and interpret the median, percentiles, quartiles and interquartile range.
E only
Key definitions
Averages
Mean = Σx / n
Median = middle value when ordered
Mode = most frequent value
Range = max − min
Cumulative frequency — Extended
LQ = lower quartile (25th percentile)
UQ = upper quartile (75th percentile)
IQR = UQ − LQ
Median = 50th percentile
